Colors Workshop

Reading: #DirectedAnalysis

I was interested to learn more about the relationship between color and emotion. Color can influence people’s current emotions and be used as an act of persuasion – companies can cater to audiences based on a color’s linked interpretation, buildings can be designed with room paint and imagery that reflects calm and peace. Colors are more powerful than the everyday user may realize. It is a non-verbal message from the product to consumers about the company’s message.

One company I noticed that does this specifically is McDonald’s. The combination of red and yellow as their sole representation not only provokes hunger, but influences their audience by showing messages of boldness, determination from the red combined with yellow’s welcoming optimism, originality, and authenticity. As I learn more about marketing, design, and color theory, it's interesting how colors can silently convey a company's identity and connect with consumers.
