Affordances & Signifiers II

Reading: #SketchInspo

The reading for Chapter 2 revolved around the role of the designer being to bridge the gap between two gulfs of a product: the desired outcome and available options. Feedback and good conceptual modeling help bridge the Gulf of Evaluation. Conceptual models are often constructed from fragmentary evidence with a poor understanding of what's happening, creating a difficult user design in the end. When users process an object to evaluate what its purpose could be, they are going through one of three levels of processing: Visceral, Behavioral, and Reflective. Visceral is making quick responses without full awareness, Behavioral is learned reactions to situations, and Reflective is deep understanding and reasoning. Product designs should be aware of all three of these processing levels, and fulfill the requirements for the user to engage in them all.

This sketch meets guidelines stated in my paragraph above in many ways. Users will quickly understand the navigation of the app, as there is a scroll bar and easy-to-understand buttons. This fits in the Visceral requirement. Behavioral is met because this layout looks similar to most modern social media sites. Finally, Reflective will be met as the app is used because moving throughout a site with this type of layout is simple and quick.
